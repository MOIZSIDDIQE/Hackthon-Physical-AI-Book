# Chapter 1 Lab: Robot Classification

## Objective

The goal of this lab is to help you understand the different types of robots and their characteristics by classifying various robotic systems based on their design, function, and application domains.

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this lab, you should be able to:

- Differentiate between various types of robots (industrial, service, humanoid, etc.)
- Identify key characteristics that define each robot type
- Understand the relationship between robot design and intended application
- Analyze the trade-offs in different robotic approaches

## Materials Needed

- Access to the internet for research
- Robot classification worksheet (provided below)
- Images and specifications of various robots (online resources)

## Background

Robot classification helps us understand the diversity in robotic systems and their specialized applications. Different robot types are optimized for specific tasks, environments, and interaction patterns. Understanding these differences is crucial for selecting appropriate robotic solutions and recognizing the unique challenges and advantages of each type.

## Discussion Questions

1. What are the main advantages of humanoid robots in human environments compared to other robot types?

2. Why might a wheeled robot be preferred over a legged robot for certain applications?

3. How do environmental constraints influence robot design choices?

4. What are the trade-offs between general-purpose and specialized robots?

5. How might advances in AI affect the classification of robots in the future?

## Extension Activities

1. **Research Project**: Choose a robot type not covered in this lab (e.g., snake robots, modular robots) and present its unique characteristics and applications to the class.

2. **Design Exercise**: Create a detailed design specification for a robot to perform a task of your choosing, explaining why you selected the particular robot type and configuration.

3. **Case Study Analysis**: Research a real-world robot deployment and analyze how well the robot type matched the application requirements.
